### **1. What is Six Sigma?**  
**Answer:**  
Six Sigma is a data-driven methodology used to reduce defects, improve quality, and make processes more efficient.

---

### **2. What does a Yellow Belt do?**  
**Answer:**  
A Yellow Belt supports improvement projects, collects data, helps in basic analysis, and assists Green/Black Belts.

---

### **3. What is DMAIC?**  
**Answer:**  
DMAIC stands for Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control â€” a structured method used to solve process problems.

---

### **4. What is a Process?**  
**Answer:**  
A process is a series of steps that convert inputs into outputs to deliver a product or service.

---

### **5. What is Variation?**  
**Answer:**  
Variation means differences in process results. Six Sigma aims to reduce variation for consistent performance.

---

### **6. What is a Defect?**  
**Answer:**  
Any result that fails to meet customer requirements or expectations.

---

### **7. What is a SIPOC Diagram?**  
**Answer:**  
SIPOC stands for Supplier, Input, Process, Output, Customer â€” a tool used to understand high-level processes.

---

### **8. What are the 7 Wastes in Lean?**  
**Answer:**  
Transport, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Overprocessing, Defects.

---

### **9. What is a Fishbone Diagram?**  
**Answer:**  
A cause-and-effect tool used to identify root causes of a problem.

---

### **10. What is a Control Chart?**  
**Answer:**  
A chart used to monitor process stability over time with upper & lower control limits.

### **12. Difference between Defect and Defective?**  
**Answer:**  
- **Defect:** A single flaw.

**Answer:**

### **21. What is Hypothesis Testing in Six Sigma?**  
**Answer:**  
A statistical method used to check if differences in process performance are due to chance or real change.

---

### **22. What is Root Cause vs. Symptom?**  
**Answer:**  
- **Symptom:** Visible issue.  
- **Root Cause:** Real reason the issue occurs.

---

### **23. Explain Control Phase Activities.**  
**Answer:**  
Create SOPs, implement control charts, train staff, and ensure improvements are sustained.

---

### **24. Difference between Special Cause & Common Cause Variation?**  
**Answer:**  
- **Common Cause:** Normal variation.  
- **Special Cause:** Unexpected variation due to specific issues.

---

### **25. What is Regression Analysis?**  
**Answer:**  
A statistical method to study relationships between variables.

---

### **26. What are Critical to Quality (CTQ) Characteristics?**  
**Answer:**  
Features that are most important to customers and define quality.

---

### **27. What is a Process Owner?**  
**Answer:**  
Person responsible for maintaining process performance after improvements.

---

### **28. What is Rolled Throughput Yield (RTY)?**  
**Answer:**  
A measure showing the probability that a product passes through all steps defect-free.

---

### **29. What is a Project Charter?**  
**Answer:**  
A document that defines the project problem, scope, goals, team, and timeline.

---

### **30. What is Value Stream Mapping?**  
**Answer:**  
A tool that visualizes the entire workflow to identify waste and improvement opportunities.
